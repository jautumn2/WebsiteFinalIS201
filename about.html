About Me

This is who I am.

In high school, I joined clubs that focused on making others feel welcome and included. I was an ambassador my junior and senior year, we were nicknamed “the nice kids club,” where our sole purpose was to reach out to those who were new and help them feel included in our school. A family friend always called me “Walking Sunshine” for my ability to light up any room I walked into and my desire to reach out to others.

One way I light up a room is my storytelling ability: finding the right inflections at the right moment, pausing for dramatic effect, forming connection between me and my audience, and building tension right before resolution. I bring those elements into the events I organize to create an immersive, unforgettable experience.

Another nickname I was given was “the Sponge.” I have a natural ability to listen to others and help them come to their own conclusions on their own. This empathetic trait has been developed further in my focused area of study in college (Experience Design and Management) by learning the importance of gaining empathy for the customer or employee. Problems aren’t solved and special moments aren’t created until we sit down and listen to our intended audience.
